# HR Analytics Dashboard Employee Attrition Analysis using Power-BI

📌 Project Overview

This Project focuses on analyzing attrition using HR data. The goal was to understand Why employees leave the company and identify the key factors that contribute to higher attrition.
I built an interactive dashboard in Power BI to explore patterns across different variables like age, salary, job role, education, and years at the company. 

🎯 Problem Statement

Which are the key factors responsible for employee attrition?

🛠 Tools Used

Power BI
- Excel / CSV
- Data Visualization
- Basic Data Analysis

📊 Key Insights

Employees with lower salaries (especially up to 5K) are more likely to leave the company, making salary one of the biggest factors behind attrition.
The 26–35 age group has the highest attrition, showing that employees in the early to mid stage of their careers tend to switch jobs more often.
Certain roles like Laboratory Technician, Sales Executive, and Research Scientist experience higher attrition compared to others.
Attrition is noticeably higher in the initial years at the company, especially during the first few years, which may indicate issues with onboarding, job satisfaction, or growth opportunities.
Employees from Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds show relatively higher attrition in comparison to other education fields.

📈 Dashboard Features

KPI cards displaying key metrics such as total employees, attrition count, attrition rate, average age, average salary, and average years at the company.
Visual breakdown of attrition based on age groups, salary slabs, job roles, education fields, and years at the company.
Use of bar charts, donut charts, and tables to clearly represent patterns and comparisons.
Highlights areas with higher attrition risk across different employee segments.
Designed with a clean and easy-to-understand layout for quick analysis and insights.

💡 Conclusion

From this analysis, I found that employee attrition is not caused by just one factor. It is mainly connected to salary, age group, job role, and how long an employee has been with the company.
Employees with lower salaries and employees in the 26–35 age group seem to leave more often. Some job roles also show higher attrition, which means those areas may need more attention from the HR team.
The dashboard also shows that many employees leave during their early years at the company. This could mean that better onboarding, career support, salary growth, and employee engagement may help reduce attrition.

Overall, this dashboard gives a clear view of where attrition is happening the most and can help HR teams take better steps to retain employees.
